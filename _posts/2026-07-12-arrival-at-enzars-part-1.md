# Setting the Scene
I haven't thought about 'how' FTL works in this universe, so I'll plot a course, make the check, and handwave the 'method.' Plotting the course will be a difficulty 2d6+1d8 (debris) test. I've probably has some experience since being on the run so: Escaped Clone distinction d8, scout d6, focus d6 (since this requires patience and concentration), Nandita d6. Failed with a hitch. That anxious stress is getting stepped back up Anxious Stress d6. As I'm brainstorming possible story developments, I draw off the GMA...catalyst is (kid you not): Vehicle breaks down. So we're going to arrive at our destination, but our vehicle is going to be in bad shape (based on the failed check).

I'll use the deck of worlds to create the opening location on Enzars (random planet name generator):

[![image of drawn cards](https://github.com/jarrodrcrawford-ops/solo-rpg-blog/blob/dbd9a5e42a3abe1b677c560d99ebbba827cb2272/assets/images/Enzars-arrival-location.jpg)]

We arrive in a crooked canyon which looks as if there were once beautiful trees that grew on outcroppings here. It looks as if there were waterfalls that once flowed down the sides of the canyon, watering the glades as they went. There is a run-down temple-looking structure (I'm thinking Druid-esque) on one of the outcroppings. The trees here have been warped and darkened (corrupted treants are going to be the vile monsters). I'm also thinking that the powerful storms attribute will not be tied to this location, alone. The planet is home to constant storms: and this is how the ship is going to end up being damaged as we arrive.

Since we're looking for a scroll, then this temple would be a good place to start.

**Scene Distinctions:**
- Roots and Thorns d6
- Storms Overhead d6
- Crooked canyon outcroppings d8

# Action of the Scene
Janus nervously sets the course for Enzars, double checking his data with Nandita. The idea of willingly flying to such a place isn't something he would have imagined himself doing a year ago. He avoided places like this, moments like this. A thought came to him...something he heard long ago, "The closer we are to danger, the further we are from harm." Perhaps there was some wisdom is knowing what was in store for him instead of running blindly from one place to another.

It seems he was nervous for nothing, as they arrived safely out of FTL in orbit over Enzars. A quick scan showed storms dotting the surface, and they couldn't penetrate beneath the storm systems. 

I can imagine the conversation between Janus and Nandita: trying to convince her to take the ship down into the storms. Nandita objecting (the ship is akin to a body for her, after all). 

I envision the ship, entering smoothly at first, then getting jostled before a larger-than-expected bolt of lightning slams into it, followed by several others.
